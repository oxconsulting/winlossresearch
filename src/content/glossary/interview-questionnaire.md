# Interview Questionnaire

An interview questionnaire is the structured set of questions used to guide a [win/loss interview](/glossary/win-loss-interview/). It's built around the client's specific GTM questions rather than a generic template, and it's what keeps interviews consistent enough across dozens of conversations to compare against each other during [pattern recognition](/glossary/pattern-recognition/).

## What a Well-Built Questionnaire Covers

A typical questionnaire moves through the buying process and internal decision discussions, the evaluation criteria the buyer actually used, competitive comparisons, pricing perceptions, whether the vendor's messaging resonated, and the specific objections or concerns that shaped the outcome. It's structured to let a buyer describe what happened rather than confirm what the interviewer already expects to hear.

## Why Sequencing Matters as Much as Content

Questions get shaped, often invisibly, by an organization's existing beliefs about why it wins and loses. A questionnaire that opens by asking directly about price will prompt buyers to confirm price as a factor, even when the underlying decision had more to do with an unaddressed evaluation criterion nobody asked about. A neutral questionnaire opens with broad, open-ended questions about the buying process before narrowing into specific topics, which gives a buyer room to volunteer the detail that actually mattered rather than defaulting to the easiest, most socially acceptable answer.

## Why It Has to Fit Inside a Short Conversation

A single interview typically runs thirty to forty-five minutes and has to cover the buying process, evaluation criteria, competitive landscape, and the specific moment things went sideways, all in that window. A questionnaire that tries to cover every possible topic exhaustively tends to produce shallow answers across all of them. One built around the client's specific GTM questions, with room to follow an unexpected thread when a buyer raises something unprompted, produces the depth that turns a passing comment into a usable finding.

## A Common Misunderstanding

An interview questionnaire is sometimes treated as interchangeable with a customer survey. The two serve different purposes. A survey collects standardized, scorable responses across a large sample. A questionnaire guides a live, one-on-one conversation and is designed to be followed off-script when a buyer raises something worth exploring further, which a survey format can't accommodate.
