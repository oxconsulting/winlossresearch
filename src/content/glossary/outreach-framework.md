# Outreach Framework

An outreach framework is the structured process used to invite and recruit former buyers for [win/loss interviews](/glossary/win-loss-interview/). It typically combines an initial email invitation, multiple follow-ups, scheduling coordination, and an [interview incentive](/glossary/interview-incentive/), and it exists because [participation rates](/glossary/participation-rate/) for win/loss interviews are low enough that reaching a program's target requires deliberate, sustained outreach rather than a single request.

## Why the Framework Has to Be This Structured

Loss interview participation typically runs around 5 percent, and win interview participation runs around 10 percent, which means hitting a target of 20 completed loss interviews requires outreach to roughly 400 former buyers, and 10 completed win interviews requires roughly 100. A single-touch invitation, sent once and never followed up, converts well below that already-modest baseline. The multi-touch structure, initial invite followed by scheduled reminders, is what closes the gap between the number of people invited and the number who actually show up.

## What the Incentive Is Actually For

An interview incentive, commonly a gift card, doesn't buy candor and isn't meant to. It offsets the time cost for a buyer who has no ongoing relationship to the vendor and no other reason to spend thirty to forty-five minutes revisiting a decision they've already moved past. Its presence in the outreach framework signals that the request is being taken seriously rather than treated as an afterthought attached to a renewal email.

## How Outreach Connects to the Timing Window

The outreach framework has to operate inside the program's [interview timing window](/glossary/interview-timing-window/), typically 30 to 90 days after a deal closes. That means outreach can't be compressed so aggressively that response rates suffer, but also can't drag on so long that the earliest interviews it produces fall outside the window where buyer memory is still reliable.

## A Common Misunderstanding

An outreach framework is sometimes treated as a marketing email sequence repurposed for research. The framing matters. A win/loss outreach email is explicit about the independence of the researcher and the purpose of the request, which is part of what makes the eventual interview credible to the buyer as a neutral conversation rather than a disguised sales or retention touchpoint.

## Why the Follow-Up Cadence Does Most of the Work

A single invitation, sent once, rarely produces the participation a program needs. Most buyers who eventually agree to an interview do so after a second or third touch, not the first, simply because the initial email arrives during a busy week and gets set aside rather than declined outright. A well-built cadence, spaced a few days apart and varying the framing slightly with each touch, recovers a meaningful share of buyers who would otherwise have been counted as non-responses.
