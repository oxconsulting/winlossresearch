# Win/Loss Interview

A win/loss interview is a structured, one-on-one conversation with a buyer, conducted after their purchase decision, designed to surface the real factors behind it. It is the primary data collection method in [win/loss research](/glossary/win-loss-research/) and the unit that every subsequent pattern is built from. One interview is a single buyer's account. A full program's worth is a dataset.

## What Happens in the Interview

A win/loss interview typically explores the buying process and internal decision discussions, the product evaluation criteria the buyer actually used, competitive comparisons, pricing perceptions, whether the vendor's messaging resonated, and the specific objections or concerns that shaped the outcome. Each program is built around a custom interview questionnaire aligned to the client's specific GTM questions, rather than a generic template applied to every engagement.

The interview is conducted by a neutral third party, not by anyone on the vendor's own sales or customer success team. That distinction is structural, not cosmetic. A buyer speaking with the vendor they just turned down manages that relationship, softening feedback and defaulting to safe, easily-explained reasons like price. A buyer speaking with someone who has no stake in the sale and no relationship to protect answers differently.

## Loss Interviews vs. Win Interviews

Programs typically weight interviews toward losses, roughly two loss interviews for every win interview. [Loss interview](/glossary/loss-interview/) findings tend to carry a cleaner signal: something specific went wrong, and buyers can usually name it. Win interviews serve a confirmatory role, testing whether a concern that surfaced repeatedly in losses also shows up in wins as something the buyer noticed but tolerated, which changes how seriously a team should treat it.

## Timing and Volume

Win/loss interviews are typically conducted within a defined window after a deal closes, close enough that the buyer's memory of specifics is intact, but far enough removed that the decision has settled and the buyer isn't still managing an active relationship. A full program usually targets 20 to 30 interviews in total. Below that, findings risk being anecdotal rather than a defensible pattern.

## A Common Misunderstanding

A win/loss interview is often confused with a customer success check-in or a sales debrief. Both of those conversations are conducted by someone with a relationship to the buyer to preserve, which shapes what gets said. A win/loss interview is conducted by someone the buyer has no reason to manage, which is what makes the account it produces different from either.
